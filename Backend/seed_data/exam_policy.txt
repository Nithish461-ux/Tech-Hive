# Examination and Assessment Policy

## Exam Schedule
- Mid-term exams: October (typically 2 weeks duration)
- End-semester exams: December/January (typically 3 weeks duration)
- Supplementary exams: July (for failed students)

## Minimum Attendance
Minimum attendance required: 75% for appearing in exams. Students with less than 75% attendance are not allowed to sit for examinations and must seek academic counseling.

## Exam Format
- Theory exams: 3 hours duration, typically worth 70% of marks
- Internal assessment: 30% based on assignments, quizzes, and class participation
- Practical exams for laboratory courses: 2-3 hours duration

## Passing Criteria
- Students must secure minimum 40% marks in theory and 40% in practical to pass a course
- For theory + practical combined courses, minimum 40% overall marks required

## Grading System
Grades are awarded based on marks secured:
- O (Outstanding): 90-100
- A+ (Excellent): 80-89
- A (Very Good): 70-79
- B+ (Good): 60-69
- B (Satisfactory): 50-59
- C (Average): 40-49
- F (Fail): Below 40

## Special Exam Rules
- Mobile phones not allowed in exam halls
- Only permitted stationery and calculators allowed
- Any form of malpractice results in immediate cancellation of results
- Medical certificate required if student is absent from exam

## Supplementary Exams
Students who fail in any course can appear for supplementary exams within the next 3 months. Maximum two attempts allowed for any course.

## Grade Point Average (GPA)
GPA is calculated based on grades secured and course credits. Cumulative GPA (CGPA) helps determine academic standing and eligibility for further studies.

## Academic Probation
Students with CGPA below 2.0 are placed on academic probation. They must improve their performance in the next semester to continue enrollment.
